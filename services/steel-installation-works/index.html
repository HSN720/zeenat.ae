The frame

Everything elsehangs off this.

Steel is the one trade whose mistakes cannot be covered. If the frame is out, the cladding is out, the glazing is out, and the first honest measurement happens too late to be cheap.

So it goes up to line and level, and the connections get detailed for the fitter who has to reach them at height — not only for the engineer who checked them at a desk.

Coastal air is the long-term enemy. Galvanising and paint systems are specified against this environment, because a frame that rusts at year eight was never a saving.

Scope

What we install

  • 01

    Structural frames

    Columns, beams and bracing erected to line and level, with connections detailed for the load and for the fitter who has to reach them.

  • 02

    Canopies & pergolas

    Entrance canopies, shading structures and pergolas where the steel is the architecture and every weld is on show.

  • 03

    Staircases & balustrades

    Feature stairs, stringers and landings, fabricated to suit finishes that arrive later without forcing a compromise.

  • 04

    Protective treatment

    Galvanising and paint systems specified against the coastal environment rather than the minimum standard.

When the frameis the finish.

Structural steel is usually buried. Architectural steel is not — a feature stair, a canopy, a pergola. There is nowhere for a poor weld to hide, and no plasterboard arriving later to forgive it.

Those get fabricated as if they will be looked at closely, because they will be.
